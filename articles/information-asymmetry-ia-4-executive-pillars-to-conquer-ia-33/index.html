November 6, 2025 To maximize data value and mitigate the risks of internal Information Asymmetry (IA), organizations must commit to these four strategic pillars

Architectural Commitment to Trust & Transparency

  • Frame all major data capital investments as explicit IA reduction projects.
  • Balance efficient centralized systems (ERP/BI) for a "single version of truth" with decentralized architectures (like Data Mesh) where centralized control risks creating expertise bottlenecks.
  • Implement immutable technologies (like DLT) for high-stakes records (finance, IP) to guarantee data traceability and eliminate Moral Hazard risk.
  • Democratize Data Access: Ensure all employees have access to relevant BI reporting that enables them to perform their work effectively and provides full visibility into the firm's strategic direction.

Institutionalizing Data Governance as a Credible Signal

  • Elevate Data Governance beyond mere compliance.
  • Use standards for quality, accuracy, and lineage to signal management reliability and trustworthiness.
  • Maintain auditable data lineage to ensure accountability and protect resource allocation decisions from adverse selection risk.

Incentivizing Cross-Functional Symmetry

  • Redesign organizational incentives and metrics to actively reward cross-functional cooperation and knowledge sharing, breaking the silo mentality.
  • Establish clear, understood KPIs and OKRs that are transparently measured.
  • Provide employees with tools to track their contributions via actionable reports, connecting individual effort directly to firm goals and results.
  • Recognize the critical role of IT business knowledge and initiate programs to systematically transfer this technical data expertise to functional business leaders.

Managing the Ethical IA Frontier

  • Establish clear, transparent policies governing how employee behavioral data and performance metrics are captured and used by BI/AI tools (internal dataveillance).
  • Proactively managing this ethical frontier preserves the trust essential for effective long-term Principal-Agent relationships and efficiency.

It is our responsibility as Leaders to effectively Combat IA.
