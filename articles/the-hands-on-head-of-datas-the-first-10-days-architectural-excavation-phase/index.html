Congratulations, you've landed the job. The C-suite expects brilliance, and your teams expect direction. If you think the first 10 days are about "listening tours" and updating your title in Slack, you're missing the point. The Head of Data role isn't an ivory tower; it's a technical leadership position built on ruthless curiosity and intimate knowledge of the bytes.

.

.

Your first two weeks aren't about proving yourself; they're about systematically removing all blind spots. This is the architectural excavation phase. Move fast, be relentlessly inquisitive, and wear steel-toed boots‚Äîyou're going into the data mines.

The Immediate Action List: Securing the Keys and the Map:

  • The Curiosity Tax

  • Map the Labyrinth

  • The Access Gauntlet

  • What Are They Reading?

  • The Top-Down Trace

  • The Stakeholder Audit

  • The Stand-Up Assimilation


Playbook

  • The Curiosity Tax: Complete initial 1:1s with your direct reports and their direct reports (if feasible). Your deliverable is 10 pages of messy, handwritten notes on key systems, people, and acronyms. You're paying your dues by listening and absorbing context fast.

  • Map the Labyrinth: Secure and review the current enterprise data architecture diagram (no matter how outdated‚Äîassume it‚Äôs wrong until proven otherwise). Identify the top 3 mission-critical data inputs and outputs. Note the updates needed. 

  • The Access Gauntlet: FULL ACCESS REQUEST SUBMITTED. Secure read/query access to all critical data systems (OLTPs, Cloud Platform, DW, Lake, BI tools). This is non-negotiable. You can't lead what you can't see, and you can't challenge assumptions without the keys.

  • "What Are They Reading?": Identify and join the top 5 executive reporting distribution lists. Trace the reports back to the underlying BI dashboards/tools. Learn the political temperature by watching what data moves the needles.

  • The Top-Down Trace: Create a simple document tracing the top 3 company KPIs (OKRs) from the executive dashboard down to the exact data fields and SQL/dbt calculations in the source systems. If you can‚Äôt trace it, you can‚Äôt trust it.

  • The Stakeholder Audit: Complete first-round 1:1 meetings with your top 3 internal business stakeholders (e.g., CFO, Head of Sales, Head of Operations). Capture their Top 3 Data Pain Points. Listen for needs disguised as wants.

  • The Stand-Up Assimilation: Attend all Data, BI, and Analytics stand-ups. Observe velocity, tooling, and perceived limitations. Your goal here is to listen, not to judge or immediately redirect. You're sensing the current engineering rhythm.

Why This Matters

Every new data leader gets handed a shiny new title and a legacy of technical debt. By focusing on these immediate, hands-on goals, you shortcut the typical political onboarding and get straight to the facts. Access is power. Understanding the architecture is the blueprint. Tracing the KPIs is grounding the strategic dialogue in reality.

Your first 10 days are done. You have access, you have initial documentation (even if it's hand-drawn on a napkin), and you know who reads what. You haven't fixed anything yet, but you've surgically removed the blind spots. Now, wipe the dust off your boots. We‚Äôre moving from observation to validation. Your next step? Run the queries yourself. The truth is in the SELECT statements, not the PowerPoint slides.
