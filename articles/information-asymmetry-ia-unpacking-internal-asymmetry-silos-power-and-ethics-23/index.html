Internal Information Asymmetry (IA) is a critical organizational challenge rooted in structural and technological imbalances. Overcoming it is key to successful, data-driven strategies like Customer Experience (CXS) and value co-creation. October 28, 2025

Data Silos Create Imbalance

  • Data silos (isolated systems and "silo mentality") are the core generator of internal IA, hoarding data and structurally impeding cross-functional collaboration.
  • The resource imbalance makes modern strategies like value co-creation inherently difficult to execute.
  • Cross-functional cooperation, particularly between IT, Analytics, Finance, Product, Operations, and Marketing teams, is essential to mitigate silos.

The Power Knowledge

The IT and "Power Users" functions holds the key knowledge that directly addresses the structural problem of silos:

  • Data Architecture: Knowing where the data resides, how it's stored, and what systems generate it.
  • Accessibility: Knowing how to connect disparate systems and securely transfer the information across departments.
  • Structure/Quality: Knowing the data's format, quality, and reliability‚Äîessential for any analytical insight to be trustworthy.

Internal Dataveillance - The New Ethical Frontier

  • Data centralization shifts the vulnerability. With Big Data raising concerns about who is consuming data. Its internal application risks disempowering the employee who contributes.
  • Firms have an ethical obligation to ensure data is used transparently and fairly, without disadvantaging the agents who contributed the data or intellectual capital.
